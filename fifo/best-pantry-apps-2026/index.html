The best pantry tracker apps in 2026

An honest comparison — from the makers of FIFO

A pantry app only helps if you actually keep using it. The two things that decide that: how little work it takes to get your food in, and whether it gives you a reason to come back. Here is an honest look at the apps worth considering in 2026 — including where each one is stronger than ours.

The short version: most pantry apps track what you own and remind you it’s expiring. FIFO adds the part nobody else does — it shows the money. You already bought the food; FIFO turns it into dinner and moves a Saved / Wasted ledger, computed from what you actually paid, to the cent.
01

How to choose

Ignore feature-count marketing. Three questions predict whether a pantry app sticks:

  • How does food get in? Manual typing is the #1 reason people quit by week two. A receipt or photo scan changes the math.
  • Does it tell you what to cook? A list that nags without helping is just guilt. The apps that last close the loop to a recipe.
  • Does it give you a reason to come back? An expiry reminder is a chore. A number that grows — money saved — is a habit.

The apps worth a look

FIFO — the one that shows the dollars

Built around a single idea: waste is abstract, but a dollar figure isn’t. Scan a grocery receipt and your pantry fills itself with prices attached; the app tells you what to use first and what to cook with it; every item you use or lose moves a real ledger priced from your own receipts. The whole pantry side — entry, expiry, recipe library, grocery list — is free, no ads, no item caps. AI receipt scanning and AI recipes are the paid tier, with a 7-day trial and a free fallback if you pass. iPhone only. Coming from NoWaste?

NoWaste — the mainstream expiry tracker

The app that made home expiry tracking mainstream. A decade of history, a mature barcode workflow, and an Android app. If manual list-keeping already works for you and you don’t need the money side or a cooking loop, it’s a solid, proven choice.

Others in the category

A wave of newer pantry and food-waste apps arrived in 2025–2026 — some focused on AI recipe suggestions, some on fridge inventory, some on surplus-food deals (a different job than tracking your own kitchen). They’re worth a look if a specific angle fits you. Check each current listing for platform, pricing, and whether it actually closes the loop from “what’s expiring” to “what’s for dinner,” since that’s where most of them stop.

Where FIFO isn’t the answer

Honesty first: FIFO is iPhone-only right now. If your household is split across platforms, an app with an Android version may settle it. FIFO is also new in 2026 — it doesn’t have a decade of reviews behind it. If you only want a simple expiring-items list and don’t care about the cost of what you throw out, a lighter app is a fine choice. The dollar tracking is the reason to pick FIFO; if that doesn’t move you, we’re probably not your app.
